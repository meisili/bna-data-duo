The Cost of Your Routine

California is the only state to require cosmetic companies to report harmful ingredients in their products. Many of these ingredients, like formaldehyde, are known to cause cancer or other harm with repeated use. Formaldehyde has been in the news for the past several years because of its toxic traits, but other ingredients are less recognizable.

Deep Dive: What's Under the Surface?

Since 2005, when the California Safe Cosmetics Act was passed into state law, over 165,000 products have been submitted to the government. 14% of these products reported have since been discontinued.

1,396 Companies
4,448 Brands
165,503 Products Submitted

Most of the products reported to California Department of Public Health were lipsticks, foundations, & eye shadows.

The number of products reported increased dramatically by nearly 350% after California required fragrance companies to report hazardous ingredients.

Since 2009, California has required cosmetic companies to report harmful ingredients in their products.

In 2022, California's Fragrance Ingredient Right to Know Act required companies to disclose fragrance ingredients for the first time — causing a dramatic surge in product submissions.
